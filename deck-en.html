Building RV Infrastructure Platform for CEE

Executive Summary

After five years of building companies from scratch to €6M revenue and break-even, we are ready to accelerate further growth through M&A. We are building an asset-backed infrastructure platform that consolidates the fragmented market of local RV (recreational vehicle) companies into the first branded multibrand network of RV service centers, dealerships and rental agencies in the Czech Republic – with subsequent expansion potential into other CEE markets (DACH, SK, PL).

There are companies on the market that can be acquired at 3–5× EBITDA. They are profitable but not growing, with neglected digitalization and branding. Through centralization of purchasing and services we will increase EBITDA margin, streamline operations through digital/AI transformation, process management, unified branding and modern customer experience. We will increase the EBITDA multiple valuation to 7–9× by creating a market leader with brand exclusivity, €60M+ revenue, 10–13% market share and EBITDA margin growth.

We will acquire premises that we lease to our own or franchise branches, combining rental income, business operations and royalties with value creation through real estate appreciation.

04. Why RVs Are Interesting
04

RV is a complex product = multiple revenue streams + customer lock-in

A motorhome combines a house (boiler, gas, heating, fridge, WC, shower) with a motor vehicle. This creates a service load 2–3× per year and a chain of upsells throughout the ownership period.

Economics of a Single RV Customer

€60K–120K typical motorhome (luxury €120K–600K).

+ 5–10% of purchase value in accessories (solar, AC, battery, mounts).

+ 2–3× annual service: spring de-winterization, autumn end-of-season, engine service.

+ ongoing repairs from minor damage – vehicle size = insurance events.

+ added services: we park, maintain and rent the RV for the owner via Campiri → flywheel.

Why RV Infrastructure Is Protected

Physical work: combination of carpentry, plumbing, electrical and engine service requires human hands and minds in the workshop.

Premises on city outskirts: hard asset that AI cannot produce more of.

Local trust: RV customers spending €80K+ want human contact and service nearby.

Regulation & certification: distribution licenses, authorized service, insurance partnerships create entry barriers.

AI as a tool, not a competitor: we use AI for pricing, leadgen, operations – it creates our edge, not a disruptor's moat.

While most venture portfolios will face AI risk in the next 5–10 years, this platform benefits from AI as operational leverage – not as a substitution threat.

07. Acquisition Strategy
07

Acquisition Strategy: OpCo + PropCo

The acquisition strategy rests on two pillars: operating companies (OpCo) and premises where companies operate (PropCo) – two complementary value-creation theses: EBITDA multiple arbitrage from operations + stable yield and appreciation from real estate.

OpCo · Operating Business
Multiple Arbitrage + EBITDA Expansion

We acquire standalone companies at a 3–5× EBITDA multiple and build a €60M+ holding market leader re-valued at 7–9× EBITDA at exit.

UpsideSingle CompanyHolding €60M
EBITDA multiple3–5×→ 7–9×
EBITDA margin5–7 %→ 10–11 %
↳ OEM rebatebaseline→ +2–3 p.p.
↳ IT / AI / marketingminimum→ +1–2 p.p.
↳ Cross-sell / up-sellad-hoc→ +1 p.p.

EBITDA multiple arbitrage: buy at 3–5× → exit at 7–9× = +2–+4 multiple for market leadership.

EBITDA expansion: margin 6% → 10% = +66% EBITDA at the same revenue.

PropCo · Real Estate Backbone
Real Estate Backbone + Stable Yield

We acquire attractive branch premises on the outskirts of major cities and lease them to OpCo – rental yield + long-term land appreciation.

Locations: outskirts of Prague and major cities, within 10–20 km of city limits. Attractive zones for logistics, retail and auto services.

Entry cap rate 5–7% – better than prime office/retail, competitive with industrial (~5.2% prime yields in CZ).

Use flexibility: RV dealership + service + rental today → backup scenario warehouse, last-mile logistics, used car lot, retail.

Hard asset: collateral for bank financing, independent of operational performance.
